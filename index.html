One engine, five playbooks

  • The Lion King — a theatre night: doors vs curtain, the dress code & strobe warning, the interval, and the last way home.
  • London Eye — a timed ride, so weather is read as visibility; it flags banned bags and the 30–60 min queue, then the SEA LIFE combo next door.
  • Tower of London — self-paced, so it sequences you to the Crown Jewels before the queue builds, times the Beefeater tour, cues the AI audio guide, and cross-sells Tower Bridge.
  • Stonehenge — built around the coach: make the 08:00 pickup, and be back before it leaves you — the strongest no-show case in the idea.
  • Colosseum — an outdoor walking tour abroad: sun, water, comfortable shoes, the skip-the-line gate, and the Roman Forum next door.
  • Bonus — a launchpad for Dex. Secondary to the core companion: at landmarks the in-venue state can launch Headout's AI guide (Dex) in one tap. A seated show skips it.
  • Cross-platform from day one. Ships as iOS Live Activities and Android Live Updates (the equivalent introduced in Android 16) — so it's not iPhone-only.
