Same pattern, three different playbooks

  • London Eye — a timed ride, so weather is read as visibility; it warns about banned bags and the 30–60 min queue, and ends on the SEA LIFE combo next door.
  • Tower of London — self-paced, so it sequences you (Crown Jewels before the queue builds), times the next Beefeater tour, cues the included AI audio guide, and cross-sells the Tower Bridge combo.
  • Stonehenge — built around the coach: the two beats that matter are making the 08:00 pickup and being back before the coach leaves you in Wiltshire — the strongest no-show case in the whole idea.
  • One engine, driven by experience type + venue rules + live weather. The card is never generic, and it never clutters past ~3 tips.
